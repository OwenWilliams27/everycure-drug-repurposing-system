The method

How MiniCure finds matches

1
Store the drugs
Each drug is stored with its approved disease in a Python dictionary — like a lookup table. Example: Metformin → Type 2 Diabetes.
→
2
Map the targets
A second dictionary records which biological targets (proteins and enzymes) each drug affects. Example: Metformin → [AMPK, mTOR].
→
3
Link targets to diseases
A third dictionary links each target to diseases that involve it. Example: AMPK → [Cancer, Alzheimer's, Aging]. This comes from research.
→
4
Follow the chain
The engine hops from drug → target → disease. If the destination disease is not the drug's approved use, it's a repurposing candidate. More shared targets = higher score.
WHY THIS MATTERS

Testing a brand-new drug from scratch takes 10–15 years and over $1 billion. A repurposed drug skips most of that — it is already proven safe in humans. Systems like this help researchers identify promising candidates much faster, especially for rare diseases where dedicated research funding is scarce.
